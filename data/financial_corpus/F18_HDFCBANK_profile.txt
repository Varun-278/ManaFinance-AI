Company: HDFC Bank Ltd.
Industry: Financial Services (Private Sector Bank)
ISIN: INE040A01034
Symbol: HDFCBANK

1. Business Overview
HDFC Bank is India's largest private-sector bank providing retail banking, corporate banking, payments, SME lending, and treasury operations. It has a nationwide branch network and rapidly expanding digital channels.

2. Revenue Drivers
- Net interest income from retail and SME loans.
- Fee income: payments, cards, cross-selling.
- Treasury operations.

3. Cost Structure
- Funding costs (CASA deposits are key).
- Credit costs from loan delinquencies.
- IT and branch expansion expenses.

4. Competitive Advantages
- Industry-leading asset quality.
- High CASA ratio lowering funding cost.
- Strong credit underwriting.

5. Key Risks
- Macro slowdowns affecting retail loan growth.
- Regulatory scrutiny post merger with HDFC.
- Competition from fintech and small finance banks.

6. Dataset Behavior
- Consistent upward trend with low volatility.
- High liquidity, strong institutional participation.

7. Financial Theory Interpretation
HDFC Bank behaves like a:
- Stable compounder,
- With valuation driven by ROE, NIM, and stable credit cost.

Its moat comes from:
- Low-cost liabilities,
- Scalable digital banking infrastructure.

8. Outlook
- Cross-selling opportunities post-HDFC merger.
- Strong traction in cards and payments ecosystem.
