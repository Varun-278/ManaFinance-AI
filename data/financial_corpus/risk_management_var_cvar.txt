RISK MANAGEMENT 

1. TYPES OF RISKS
• Market risk (equity, FX, rates, commodities)
• Credit risk (default + downgrade)
• Liquidity risk
• Operational risk
• Systemic risk

2. VALUE AT RISK (VAR)
VAR answers:
“What is the worst expected loss over a given period at a given confidence level?”

Methods:
• Historical VAR
• Variance-Covariance (parametric)
• Monte Carlo simulation

Formula (Parametric):
VAR = Z * σ * √t * Portfolio Value

3. CONDITIONAL VAR (CVaR)
Measures expected loss *beyond* the VAR threshold.
CVaR > VAR → captures tail risk.

4. STRESS TESTING
Focus areas:
• Interest rate shocks
• Oil price spikes
• INR depreciation
• Liquidity freeze scenarios

5. CREDIT RISK MODELLING
Tools:
• PD (Probability of Default)
• LGD (Loss Given Default)
• EAD (Exposure at Default)

Expected Credit Loss:
ECL = PD × LGD × EAD

6. RISK IN BANKS & NBFCs
Driven by:
• Asset-liability mismatch
• NPA cycles
• Funding cost sensitivity
• Concentration of exposures

7. HEDGING TECHNIQUES
• Futures hedging (index, currency, commodity)
• Options hedging (protective put, collars)
• Duration matching (bonds)
• FX swaps
