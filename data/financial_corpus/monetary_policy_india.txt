MONETARY POLICY IN INDIA 

1. OBJECTIVE OF RBI MONETARY POLICY
The Reserve Bank of India's primary mandate is to maintain price stability while supporting growth.
India follows a Flexible Inflation Targeting (FIT) framework with a mandated target of:
- CPI inflation at 4%
- Tolerance band: ±2%

2. MONETARY POLICY COMMITTEE (MPC)
The MPC is a 6-member committee:
- 3 RBI officials
- 3 Government-appointed external members
Decisions are taken by majority vote and announced bi-monthly.

3. KEY POLICY INSTRUMENTS
a) Repo Rate  
   - The rate at which RBI lends overnight funds to banks.
b) Reverse Repo Rate  
   - The rate at which banks park excess liquidity with RBI.
c) Standing Deposit Facility (SDF)  
   - Introduced to absorb liquidity without needing collateral.
d) Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)  
   - % of bank deposits held with RBI.
e) Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR)  
   - Minimum liquid assets banks must maintain.
f) Open Market Operations (OMO)  
   - RBI buys/sells government securities to influence liquidity.
g) Market Stabilization Scheme (MSS)  
   - Used during excess capital flows.

4. TRANSMISSION MECHANISM
The impact of rate changes depends on:
- Banking sector health
- Credit demand
- Liquidity conditions
- Government borrowing program
Transmission channels include:
- Interest rate channel
- Credit/Bank lending channel
- Asset price channel
- Expectations channel

5. LIQUIDITY MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK
Liquidity Adjustment Facility (LAF) provides tools to adjust short-term liquidity.
RBI also uses variable rate reverse repo (VRRR) auctions to absorb surplus liquidity.

6. POLICY REACTION FUNCTION
RBI’s decision-making depends on:
- Inflation trajectory vs target
- Output gap
- Financial stability risks
- Global monetary conditions (especially US Federal Reserve)
- Oil and commodity price trends

7. MONETARY POLICY AND THE RUPEE
Monetary tightening supports currency stability.
Loose monetary policy can cause depreciation pressures.

8. CHALLENGES
- Weak transmission in public sector bank-dominated system
- Large informal sector
- Supply-side inflation (food & fuel)
- Global spillovers from foreign monetary policy

9. FUTURE DIRECTION
- Greater reliance on forward guidance
- Development of the Indian corporate bond market
- Integration with global interest rate cycles
