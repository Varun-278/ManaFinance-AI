Company: Bajaj Finance Ltd.
Industry: Financial Services (NBFC – Consumer & SME Lending)
ISIN: INE296A01024
Symbol: BAJFINANCE

1. Business Overview
Bajaj Finance is one of India’s largest and most profitable non-banking financial companies (NBFCs), specializing in consumer durable loans, personal loans, co-branded credit cards, SME financing, digital loans, and commercial lending. The company operates through a highly diversified and data-driven lending model backed by risk analytics.

2. Revenue Drivers
- Interest income from consumer durable financing and personal loans.
- SME and commercial lending.
- Credit card fees (via co-branded partnerships).
- Cross-sell through existing customer base.

3. Cost Structure
- Cost of funds (bank borrowings + NCDs).
- Credit costs based on delinquency trends.
- Operating expenses (technology and branch expansion).
- Marketing and customer acquisition.

4. Competitive Advantage
- One of the strongest risk-assessment engines among NBFCs.
- Very high cross-sell efficiency due to large customer pool.
- Fast loan disbursal turnaround (digital-first model).
- High-quality co-branded card portfolio.

5. Key Risks
- Asset quality sensitivity during economic downturns.
- Regulatory constraints on NBFC leverage.
- Rising competition from fintechs and small finance banks.

6. Stock Behavior Insights (from dataset)
- High sensitivity to macro interest rate cycles.
- Sudden volume surges around quarterly results.
- Strong long-term uptrend behavior typical of high-growth financials.

7. Financial Theory Interpretation
Bajaj Finance mimics characteristics of:
- A growth stock (high P/E multiples),
- A leveraged financial firm (sensitive to credit cycles).

Valuation is driven by:
- AUM growth,
- Net interest margins (NIM),
- Asset quality (NPA trends),
- Cost-to-income ratio.

8. Outlook Themes
- Transition toward a full digital bank architecture.
- Expansion into payments and deposits via Bajaj Finserv’s ecosystem.
- Improved diversification of lending book.
