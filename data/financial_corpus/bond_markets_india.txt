THE INDIAN BOND MARKET 

1. STRUCTURE OF THE BOND MARKET
Major segments:
- Government securities (G-secs)
- State development loans (SDLs)
- Corporate bonds
- Municipal bonds
- Treasury bills
- Commercial paper & certificates of deposit

2. GOVERNMENT SECURITIES (G-SECS)
The largest and most liquid part of India’s bond market.
Key benchmarks: 10-year G-sec yield.

Factors affecting G-sec yields:
- Inflation expectations
- RBI policy stance
- Government borrowing
- Global yields (especially US 10-year)

3. CORPORATE BOND MARKET
Categories:
- AAA to junk-rated bonds
- Public issue vs private placement
- Infrastructure bonds
- Perpetual bonds (AT1)

Challenges:
- Limited liquidity
- Narrow investor base
- High credit risk perception

4. YIELD CURVE
The yield curve reflects expectations of:
- Growth
- Inflation
- Policy path

An inverted yield curve may signal recessionary expectations.

5. CREDIT RISK & SPREADS
Corporate bonds trade at spreads over G-secs depending on:
- Business risk
- Financial health
- Sector exposure  
- Liquidity

6. RBI’S ROLE
RBI influences bond markets via:
- G-sec auctions
- OMO operations
- HTM (Held-to-Maturity) relaxations
- Liquidity management operations

7. FOREIGN PARTICIPATION
FPI participation limited due to:
- Regulatory limits
- Yield volatility
- Currency risk
