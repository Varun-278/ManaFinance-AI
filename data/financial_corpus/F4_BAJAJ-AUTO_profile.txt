1. Business Overview
Bajaj Auto is a global two-wheeler and three-wheeler manufacturer with significant exports. The company’s core portfolio spans motorcycles (Pulsar, Boxer, KTM), scooters, and commercial three-wheelers.

2. Revenue Drivers
- Domestic two-wheeler sales.
- Export sales to Africa, Latin America, and Asia.
- Market share in premium motorcycles.
- Three-wheeler demand cycles.

3. Cost Structure
- Commodity-linked raw materials (steel, rubber, aluminum).
- R&D investment in EV and performance bikes.
- Manufacturing and distribution costs.

4. Competitive Advantages
- Strong brand presence in premium motorcycle segment.
- Cost-efficient manufacturing.
- Dominant exporter in two-wheelers.

5. Risks
- Currency fluctuations impacting export revenue.
- Demand cyclicality in rural and export markets.
- Competition from EV entrants.

6. Dataset-Based Stock Behavior
- Noticeable price momentum around quarterly auto sales data.
- Exports-driven cycles show up in volatility clusters.
- Generally moderate volatility.

7. Financial Theory Lens
As a cyclical consumer manufacturer, Bajaj Auto’s valuation depends on:
- Economic recovery,
- Commodity cycles,
- Export market stability.

8. Outlook
- Electric two-wheeler scale-up.
- Strengthening premium product mix.
