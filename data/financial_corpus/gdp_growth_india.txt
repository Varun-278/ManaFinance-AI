GDP GROWTH IN INDIA 

1. GDP MEASUREMENT
GDP = C + I + G + (X – M)
- C: Private consumption
- I: Investment
- G: Government expenditure
- X – M: Net exports

2. GROWTH DRIVERS
- Consumption (~57% of GDP)
- Public capital expenditure
- Demographics (young population)
- Services exports
- Digital infrastructure

3. CYCLICAL VS STRUCTURAL GROWTH
Cyclical factors:
- Monetary policy cycles
- Commodity cycles
- Monsoon variability

Structural factors:
- Productivity improvements  
- Reforms (GST, IBC)  
- Urbanisation  
- Financialisation  

4. TOTAL FACTOR PRODUCTIVITY (TFP)
India’s TFP growth improves with:
- Digital adoption
- Efficient logistics
- Formalisation of MSMEs  
- Labour reforms

5. INVESTMENT CYCLE DYNAMICS
Investment revival depends on:
- Corporate deleveraging  
- Bank balance sheet repair  
- Policy stability  
- Ease of doing business  

6. EXPORT COMPETITIVENESS
Key export areas:
- IT Services
- Pharma
- Engineering goods
- Chemicals
- Agriculture commodities

7. CHALLENGES
- Low female labour force participation  
- Skill mismatch  
- High logistics costs  
- Low manufacturing share (~17% of GDP)

8. LONG-TERM OUTLOOK
GDP can reach $5 trillion with:
- Strong manufacturing push  
- Higher investment-to-GDP ratio  
- Human capital development
