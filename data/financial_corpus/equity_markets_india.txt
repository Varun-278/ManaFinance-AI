EQUITY MARKETS IN INDIA

1. ROLE OF EQUITY MARKETS
Equity markets facilitate capital formation by allowing companies to raise funds through public
offerings. They improve liquidity, price discovery, and resource allocation efficiency.

2. PRIMARY MARKET
Key components:
- IPO (Initial Public Offering)
- FPO (Follow-on Public Offering)
- Rights issues
- QIP (Qualified Institutional Placement)
- Book building mechanism

3. SECONDARY MARKET
The secondary market enables trading of listed securities.  
Key factors influencing secondary market performance:
- Corporate earnings  
- Monetary policy  
- Global macro conditions  
- FPI flows  
- Sector rotations  

4. MARKET MICROSTRUCTURE
Indian equities use a fully electronic limit order book.

Key elements:
- Bid/ask spread  
- Market depth  
- Tick size  
- Impact cost  
- Latency  

5. FUNDAMENTAL ANALYSIS FACTORS
Analysts evaluate:
- Revenue growth  
- Profit margins  
- ROE/ROCE  
- Cash flows  
- Competitive moats (moat analysis)  
- Management quality  

6. TECHNICAL ANALYSIS FACTORS
Traders analyze:
- Price action  
- Support / resistance  
- Moving averages  
- Volatility  
- Volume patterns  
- Breakouts and trend reversals  

7. MARKET STYLE FACTORS
Common equity factors:
- Value  
- Growth  
- Momentum  
- Quality  
- Volatility  

8. RISKS
- Liquidity risk  
- Earnings shocks  
- Geopolitical shocks  
- Regulatory changes
