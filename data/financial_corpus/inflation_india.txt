INFLATION IN INDIA

1. CPI (PRIMARY MEASURE)
India uses CPI (Consumer Price Index) as the primary inflation indicator.
CPI basket weights:
- Food & beverages ~ 46%
- Housing ~ 10%
- Fuel & light ~ 7%
- Core inflation (ex-food & fuel) ~ 40%

2. TYPES OF INFLATION
a) Demand-pull inflation  
b) Cost-push inflation  
c) Structural inflation  
d) Imported inflation  
e) Food inflation (highly volatile, monsoon-driven)

3. INFLATION DIFFERENTIAL: INDIA VS WORLD
India’s inflation is more sensitive to:
- Food shocks
- Crude oil imports
- Supply chain disruptions
- Fiscal policy

4. ROLE OF RBI
RBI targets 4% ± 2%.  
Tools:
- Repo rate
- CRR, SLR
- Liquidity operations
- Communication/forward guidance

5. CORE VS HEADLINE INFLATION
Core inflation reflects underlying demand.
Headline inflation includes volatile food & fuel components.

6. DRIVERS OF INFLATION (2020–2024 Examples)
- Covid-induced supply disruptions
- Global commodity super cycle
- Russia–Ukraine conflict affecting crude and fertilizers
- El Niño–induced food inflation

7. MEASURING INFLATION EXPECTATIONS
Indicators:
- Household surveys
- G-sec inflation-indexed yields
- Corporate price-setting behavior

8. ANTI-INFLATION POLICY OPTIONS
- Monetary tightening
- Fiscal consolidation
- Food supply management
- Import duty adjustments
- Export controls (temporary)

9. LONG-TERM RISKS
- Climate impact on food  
- Rupee depreciation  
- Energy security
