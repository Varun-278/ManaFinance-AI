INTEREST RATES & MONETARY POLICY 

1. ROLE OF RBI
RBI regulates liquidity and borrowing costs to maintain:
• Price stability (CPI target 4% ± 2%)
• Economic growth
• Financial stability

2. KEY POLICY RATES
Repo Rate:
Rate at which RBI lends short-term funds to banks.
↑ Repo → expensive credit → lower inflation.

Reverse Repo:
Rate at which banks park excess funds with RBI.

MSF (Marginal Standing Facility):
Emergency borrowing window for banks.

Bank Rate:
Long-term signaling rate.

3. LIQUIDITY MANAGEMENT
RBI uses:
• LAF corridor
• Open Market Operations (OMO)
• Variable Rate Reverse Repo (VRRR)
• Variable Rate Repo (VRR)
• CRR changes

4. MONETARY TRANSMISSION
Channels:
• Interest rate channel
• Credit channel
• Exchange rate channel
• Asset price channel
• Expectations channel

5. RBI’S POLICY FRAMEWORK (MPC)
Monetary Policy Committee meets bi-monthly to set repo rate.
Members: 3 RBI + 3 Govt.

6. IMPACT OF RATE CHANGES
Rate Hike:
• Loan EMIs rise
• Investment slows
• Demand cools
• Rupee strengthens (typically)
• Bond yields rise

Rate Cut:
• Credit growth increases
• Asset prices rise
• Rupee may weaken
• Consumption improves

7. STRUCTURAL ISSUES
• Transmission delays in Indian banking
• NPA cycles affect credit growth
• High government borrowing impacts yields
