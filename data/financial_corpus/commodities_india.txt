COMMODITY MARKETS IN INDIA 

1. MAJOR COMMODITY EXCHANGES
- MCX (Multi Commodity Exchange)
- NCDEX (National Commodity & Derivatives Exchange)

2. COMMODITY TYPES
- Metals: gold, silver, copper, aluminium
- Energy: crude oil, natural gas
- Agricultural: wheat, soybean, spices, cotton

3. DRIVERS OF COMMODITY PRICES
- Global demand–supply imbalances  
- Geopolitical tensions  
- Currency movements  
- Inventory cycles  
- Climatic conditions (for agri products)

4. COMMODITY DERIVATIVES
Futures are the most traded.  
Options introduced in recent years.

5. CRUDE OIL IMPACT ON INDIA
- India imports ~85% of crude  
- Oil prices affect inflation, fiscal deficit, CAD, rupee value  
- Large macro spillover effects  

6. GOLD MARKET DYNAMICS
Gold demand driven by:
- Weddings & culture  
- Safe-haven demand  
- Rupee depreciation  
- Global uncertainty  

7. AGRI COMMODITIES
Influenced by:
- Monsoon  
- MSP policies  
- Storage and logistics  
- Global crop conditions
