EXTERNAL SECTOR — INDIA’S INTEGRATION WITH THE GLOBAL ECONOMY

1. CURRENT ACCOUNT (CA)
CA includes:
- Trade balance (goods)
- Invisibles (services + remittances)
- Income payments

India usually runs a CA deficit due to:
- High crude oil imports
- Gold imports
- Electronics and machinery imports

2. FOREIGN EXCHANGE RESERVES
India’s forex reserves > USD 600 billion.
Reserves cover:
- Import adequacy (10+ months)
- Short-term external debt
- Market interventions

3. CAPITAL FLOWS
Two major types:
- FDI (more stable)
- FPI (volatile)

FPI flows affect equity and bond market volatility.

4. EXCHANGE RATE MANAGEMENT
RBI follows a managed float:
- Intervenes during extreme volatility
- Allows market-driven adjustments

5. REMITTANCES
India is world’s top remittance receiver (~USD 100 billion).
Helps support CAD.

6. SERVICES EXPORTS
Key sectors:
- IT Services
- Consulting
- Financial services
- Healthcare outsourcing

7. RISKS
- Oil price shocks
- Global recession
- US monetary tightening
- Geo-political disruptions
