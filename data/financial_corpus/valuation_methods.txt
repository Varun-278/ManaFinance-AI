VALUATION METHODS

1. INTRINSIC VALUATION METHODS
a) Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
DCF = Σ (Free Cash Flow / (1 + WACC)^t) + Terminal Value
Terminal value approaches:
- Perpetuity growth model
- Exit multiple model

b) Dividend Discount Model (DDM)
Applicable for stable dividend-paying firms.

c) Residual Income Model (Economic Profit)
Value = Book Value + Σ (Residual Income discounted)

2. RELATIVE VALUATION (MULTIPLES)
Common multiples:
- P/E (Price / Earnings)
- EV/EBITDA
- EV/Sales
- P/BV
- PEG ratio
- Price-to-Cash Flow

Benchmarking:
- Compare with peers
- Compare with historical levels
- Adjust for business cycle

3. SUM OF THE PARTS (SOTP)
Used for conglomerates.
Value = Σ (Value of each business unit)

4. NET ASSET VALUE (NAV)
Used for:
- Real estate companies
- Investment holding firms
- REITs and InvITs

5. OPTION-BASED VALUATION
Real options approach used when:
- Projects have flexibility
- Commodity-driven valuations
- Tech startups

6. STARTUP VALUATION
Methods:
- Venture Capital method
- Scorecard valuation
- Berkus method
- Market multiples

7. SECTOR-SPECIFIC VALUATION
Banking:
- P/BV and ROE-based valuation
- Gordon Growth Model

IT Services:
- EV/EBITDA
- Revenue multiples for fast-growth firms

Pharma:
- Risk-adjusted NPV for drug pipelines

Energy:
- NAV + long-term cash flows

8. KEY VALUATION DRIVERS
- Growth
- Profitability
- Margins
- Cash flow generation
- Competitive advantage (moat)
- Cost of capital
