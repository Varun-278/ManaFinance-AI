INDIAN STOCK MARKET OVERVIEW 

1. STRUCTURE OF THE INDIAN CAPITAL MARKETS
India’s capital market ecosystem comprises:
- Equity markets (cash + derivatives)
- Debt markets (government + corporate bonds)
- Commodity markets (via MCX, NCDEX)
- Currency markets
- Alternative investment markets (AIFs, REITs, InvITs)

Two major exchanges dominate:
- NSE (National Stock Exchange)
- BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange)

2. MARKET PARTICIPANTS
Key players:
- Retail investors
- High Net Worth Individuals (HNIs)
- Domestic Institutional Investors (DIIs)
- Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs)
- Mutual funds, pension funds
- Market makers & brokers
- Algo/quant traders

3. MARKET SEGMENTS
a) Cash market  
b) Equity derivatives (Index & Stock Futures/Options)  
c) Currency derivatives  
d) Interest rate futures  
e) Commodity derivatives  

4. INDICES
Key benchmark indices:
- NIFTY50  
- SENSEX  
- NIFTY BANK  
- NIFTY IT  
- NIFTY METAL  
- Sectoral & thematic indices

Indices help measure market sentiment, sector performance, and benchmark portfolio returns.

5. PRICE DISCOVERY MECHANISM
India uses anonymous order-driven electronic trading.  
Price formation depends on:
- Order flows  
- Liquidity depth  
- Information assimilation  
- Market microstructure  
- Global cues  

6. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
SEBI ensures:
- Market integrity  
- Corporate governance  
- Fair disclosure  
- Surveillance against manipulation  

7. SETTLEMENT
India operates a T+1 settlement cycle for equities.

8. RECENT TRENDS
- Rise of retail participation post-2020  
- Growth of passive funds  
- Algo trading & co-location  
- Sustainable finance (ESG funds)  
- Rapid growth in derivatives turnover
