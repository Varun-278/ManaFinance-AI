BALANCE SHEET ANALYSIS 

1. OVERVIEW
The balance sheet reveals liquidity position, financial strength, leverage, capital structure, and
resource allocation efficiency.

2. ASSET COMPONENTS
a) Current assets  
   - Cash & equivalents  
   - Inventory  
   - Receivables  
   - Short-term investments  

b) Non-current assets  
   - Property, Plant & Equipment  
   - Intangibles  
   - Goodwill  
   - Deferred tax assets  

3. LIABILITY COMPONENTS
a) Current liabilities  
   - Payables  
   - Short-term borrowings  
   - Accrued expenses  

b) Long-term liabilities  
   - Long-term debt  
   - Lease liabilities  
   - Provision obligations  

4. EQUITY COMPONENTS
- Share capital  
- Share premium  
- Reserves  
- Retained earnings  

5. KEY RATIOS
a) Liquidity ratios  
   Current Ratio = CA / CL  
   Quick Ratio = (CA – Inventory) / CL  

b) Leverage ratios  
   Debt/Equity  
   Debt/EBITDA  
   Interest Coverage  

c) Asset quality  
   Net fixed assets turnover  
   Return on assets (ROA)  

6. ANALYTICAL INSIGHTS
- Excessive leverage increases bankruptcy risk  
- High receivables may signal credit policy issues  
- Inventory build-up may indicate weak demand
