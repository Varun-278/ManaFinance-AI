Company: State Bank of India
Industry: Banking (Public Sector)
ISIN: INE062A01020
Symbol: SBIN

1. Business Overview
SBI is India’s largest bank with massive branch presence, corporate and retail lending, treasury operations, and subsidiaries in insurance, mutual funds, and cards.

2. Revenue Drivers
- Retail and corporate loan book.
- Treasury income.
- Fee income from payments, cards, and distribution.

3. Cost Structure
- Deposits interest costs.
- Credit provisions (large due to corporate exposure).
- Employee expenses (higher vs private peers).

4. Competitive Advantages
- Largest deposit franchise in India.
- Strong brand trust.
- CASA dominance enabling lower funding costs.

5. Risks
- Cyclicality in corporate NPAs.
- PSU governance limitations.
- Competition from private banks.

6. Dataset Behavior
- High liquidity stock,
- Strong cyclical uptrend during credit growth cycles,
- Higher beta than private banks.

7. Financial Theory Interpretation
Valuation depends on:
- Credit cycle improvement,
- GNPA reductions,
- ROE expansion.

8. Outlook
- Strong retail loan growth,
- Improvement in asset quality,
- Digital transformation strengthening efficiency.
