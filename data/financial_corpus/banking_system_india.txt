BANKING SYSTEM OF INDIA 

1. STRUCTURE OF INDIAN BANKING
Banks in India include:
- Public Sector Banks (PSBs)
- Private Sector Banks
- Foreign Banks
- Small Finance Banks
- Payments Banks
- Cooperative Banks

2. ROLE OF BANKS
Banks perform:
- Credit intermediation
- Payment services
- Liquidity management
- Financial inclusion

3. BANK BALANCE SHEET STRUCTURE
Assets:
- Loans & advances
- Investments (G-Secs)
- Cash & reserves

Liabilities:
- Deposits (CASA + Term)
- Borrowings
- Capital & reserves

4. NON-PERFORMING ASSETS (NPAs)
NPA = loan overdue > 90 days  
Impact:
- Reduces profitability
- Raises provisioning requirements
- Restricts new lending

Resolution mechanisms:
- Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC)
- SARFAESI Act
- Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs)

5. CAPITAL ADEQUACY
Banks must maintain:
- CRAR as per Basel III norms
- Tier 1 & Tier 2 capital requirements
- Leverage ratio norms

6. LIQUIDITY REGULATION
Mandatory:
- CRR
- SLR
- Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)

7. DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION
India’s digital stack enables:
- UPI-based payments
- Aadhaar e-KYC
- Direct Benefit Transfers (DBT)
- Digital lending

8. RISKS IN BANKING
- Credit risk
- Market risk
- Operational risk
- Liquidity risk
- Cybersecurity threats

9. FUTURE TRENDS
- AI-driven lending
- Open banking (account aggregators)
- Consolidation among public sector banks
- Growth of neo-banking models
