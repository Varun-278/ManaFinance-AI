INFLATION IN INDIA — CPI, WPI, DRIVERS, MACRO RELATIONSHIPS

1. TYPES OF INFLATION MEASURES
CPI (Consumer Price Index)
• Measures retail inflation
• Used for monetary policy targeting
• Components: Food, Fuel, Core (ex-food & fuel)

WPI (Wholesale Price Index)
• Measures producer-level prices
• Sensitive to commodities & global prices

GDP Deflator
• Measures overall price changes in economy

2. DRIVERS OF INFLATION IN INDIA
Demand-pull inflation:
• Excess consumer demand
• High credit growth

Cost-push inflation:
• Commodity prices (oil)
• Supply chain constraints
• Weak monsoons affecting food prices

Imported inflation:
• Depreciating rupee increases import costs

3. CORE VS HEADLINE
Headline CPI includes food and fuel (volatile)
Core CPI removes them and reflects structural pressures.

4. RBI'S RESPONSE
RBI uses repo rate increases when:
• Core inflation sticky
• Food inflation spills over
• Demand overheating occurs

5. RELATIONSHIP WITH GROWTH
• Mild inflation encourages investment
• High inflation → lower real incomes
• Disinflation phase boosts consumption

6. INFLATION EXPECTATIONS
Anchoring expectations is crucial.
RBI influences expectations through:
• Forward guidance
• Consistent policy stance
