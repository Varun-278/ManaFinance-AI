ADVANCED VALUATION METHODS 

1. INTRINSIC VALUATION
a. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
DCF = Σ (FCF_t / (1 + WACC)^t) + Terminal Value
Key Inputs:
• Revenue growth assumptions
• Operating margins
• CAPEX & reinvestment rate
• Working capital needs
• WACC (Cost of equity + cost of debt)

Terminal Value Models:
• Perpetuity Growth (Gordon Model)
• Exit multiple method

b. Dividend Discount Model (DDM)
Applicable for:
• Mature companies
• Banks & NBFCs
Value = Σ (Dividends / (1 + k)^t)

2. RELATIVE VALUATION
Key Multiples:
• P/E (price-to-earnings)
• EV/EBITDA
• P/B (best for banks)
• EV/Sales (early-stage companies)

Drivers of multiples:
• ROE, ROCE
• Growth rate
• Risk profile
• Industry structure

3. ASSET-BASED VALUATION
Used for:
• Holding companies
• Distress situations
• Real estate companies
NAV = Sum of fair value of assets – liabilities

4. SUM OF THE PARTS (SOTP)
Used when company has:
• Multiple unrelated business units
• Conglomerates (ex: Reliance, Tata)

5. VALUATION OF FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS
Banks → P/B + ROE + GNPA/NNPA analysis  
NBFCs → NIM, AUM growth, cost-to-income, credit cost

6. VALUATION CHALLENGES
• Cyclical earnings  
• Commodities unpredictability  
• Regulatory changes  
• High interest rate sensitivity  
• FX-linked volatility  

7. MARKET EFFICIENCY TOPICS
Semi-strong form EMH → Prices reflect publicly available info  
But anomalies exist:
• Value vs growth cycles
• Momentum effects
• Earnings drift
