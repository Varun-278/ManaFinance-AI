Company: Housing Development Finance Corporation Ltd.
Industry: Financial Services (Housing Finance)
ISIN: INE001A01036
Symbol: HDFC

1. Business Overview
HDFC (before merging with HDFC Bank) was India's largest housing finance institution focused on retail home loans, project loans, and affordable housing financing.

2. Revenue Drivers
- Interest income on home loans.
- Fee income from insurance and investment products.
- Spread between lending rates and borrowing costs.

3. Cost Structure
- Borrowing costs (NCDs, deposits via subsidiaries).
- Credit provisions for NPAs.
- Operating expenses (distribution and origination).

4. Competitive Strengths
- Very low NPA levels historically.
- Strong underwriting discipline.
- Large retail customer base with high repayment behavior.

5. Risks
- Sensitivity to interest rate cycles.
- Real estate sector cyclicality.
- Competition from banks and NBFCs.

6. Dataset Behavior
- Low volatility, stable uptrend consistent with financial stability.
- Volume pickups around merger-related announcements.

7. Financial Theory Interpretation
HDFC resembles:
- A mortgage REIT-like lender with stable cash flows,
- Spread-based business (lending–borrowing margin).

Key valuation metrics:
- Net interest margin (NIM),
- Loan book growth,
- Asset quality.

8. Outlook
- Integration benefits with HDFC Bank.
- Scale-driven efficiency improvements.
