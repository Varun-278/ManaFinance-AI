Company: Bharti Infratel Ltd. (merged into Indus Towers)
Industry: Telecom Infrastructure
ISIN: INE121J01017
Symbol: INFRATEL

1. Business Overview
Infratel (prior to merging with Indus Towers) was a telecom tower infrastructure provider offering passive infrastructure to mobile operators for network rollout.

2. Revenue Model
- Rental income from telecom operators.
- Co-location revenue (multiple tenants per tower).
- Energy reimbursement income.

3. Cost Structure
- Tower maintenance and energy cost.
- Ground lease rentals.
- Depreciation (large due to tower assets).

4. Competitive Strength
- One of India’s largest tower networks.
- Long-term tenancy contracts ensure stable annuity revenues.
- Synergy benefits post-merger with Indus Towers.

5. Risks
- Tenant exits (e.g., Vodafone Idea weakness).
- 5G rollout shifting economics of tower sharing.
- High dependency on few major clients.

6. Dataset Observations
- Moderate volatility.
- Volume changes aligned with telecom sector announcements.

7. Financial Theory Interpretation
Infratel behaves like:
- A yield-oriented quasi-REIT,
- With valuation driven by tenancy ratios,
- And EBITDA margins.

8. Outlook
- Consolidation in telecom aiding stable tenancies.
- Potential inclusion in digital infra growth.
