Company: Coal India Ltd.
Industry: Metals & Mining (Coal Mining)
ISIN: INE522F01014
Symbol: COALINDIA

1. Business Overview
Coal India is the world’s largest coal producer, supplying ~80% of India's domestic coal requirement. It operates through subsidiaries engaged in mining, beneficiation, and transportation of coal used in power generation, steel production, and other industrial processes.

2. Revenue Drivers
- Coal production volumes.
- Realisations from FSA (Fuel Supply Agreements).
- E-auction premiums (volatile but high-margin).
- Linkage with power plants and state utilities.

3. Cost Structure
- Labor costs (very high due to large workforce).
- Overburden removal and mining operations.
- Logistics, material handling.
- Mine development and safety expenses.

4. Competitive Advantages
- Near-monopoly in domestic coal supply.
- Long-term contracting with power plants ensures stable demand.
- Strong cash generation and high dividend payouts.

5. Key Risks
- Transition toward renewable energy reducing long-term demand.
- Regulatory scrutiny on mining practices.
- Dependence on monsoons (affects mining productivity).

6. Dataset-Based Observations
- Typically low volatility with steady volumes.
- Price action shows mean-reversion due to stable fundamentals.
- Rare but sharp swings linked to government policy announcements.

7. Financial Theory Interpretation
As a quasi-monopoly public sector enterprise (PSE), Coal India exhibits:
- Predictable cash flows,
- High dividend yield,
- Limited growth optionality.

Valuation is income-based (Dividend Discount / Cash Flow Stability).

8. Outlook
- Gradual diversification into coal gasification.
- Efficiency modernization using mechanized mining.
