GDP GROWTH DRIVERS IN INDIA 

1. GDP STRUCTURE
GDP = C + I + G + (X – M)
Where:
C = Consumption (largest component)
I = Investment
G = Government spending
X – M = Net exports

2. MAJOR GROWTH DRIVERS
Consumption:
• Stable due to demographic profile
• Rural vs urban divergence

Investment:
• Driven by capacity utilisation
• Private capex cycles
• FDI flows

Government Spending:
• Infrastructure push
• Welfare schemes

Exports:
• IT services
• Pharma
• Engineering goods
• Commodity cycles influence performance

3. CYCLICAL DRIVERS
• Credit cycles
• Business confidence
• Global demand
• Energy prices
• Monsoon impact on agriculture

4. STRUCTURAL DRIVERS
• Digital economy
• Manufacturing reforms (PLI schemes)
• Urbanization
• Formalization of economy
• Labour reforms

5. RISKS TO GROWTH
• Global recession
• High interest rates
• Fiscal tightening
• Banking system stress
• Commodity shocks
