MACROECONOMICS OF INDIA 

1. STRUCTURE OF THE INDIAN ECONOMY
India is a structurally diverse emerging market economy with three major sectors:
• Primary (Agriculture & allied activities)
• Secondary (Manufacturing & industry)
• Tertiary (Services — dominant contributor)

Current GDP composition (approx):
Agriculture: 15%
Industry: 29%
Services: 56%

2. KEY MACRO DRIVERS
Growth is driven by:
• Domestic consumption (bulk of GDP)
• Government expenditure
• Private investment
• Export performance
• Financial sector stability
• Policy credibility (RBI + fiscal policy)

3. FISCAL POLICY IN INDIA
Government manages economic activity through:
• Union Budget
• Direct/indirect taxes
• Subsidies & transfer schemes
• Infrastructure spending

Fiscal Deficit:
FD ≈ (Total Expenditure – Total Receipts) / GDP
High deficits → inflationary pressure, higher yields.

4. RBI’S ROLE IN MACROECONOMIC STABILITY
RBI influences:
• Inflation
• Liquidity
• Credit conditions
• Rupee stability
• Financial market regulation

RBI Tools:
• Repo rate
• CRR
• SLR
• OMOs
• Standing facilities

5. BUSINESS CYCLE IN INDIA
India cycles through phases:
• Expansion → strong credit, high consumption
• Peak → inflation rises, RBI tightens policy
• Slowdown → investment weakens
• Recovery → policy easing + improving demand

6. STRUCTURAL CHALLENGES
• Labour market rigidity
• Low manufacturing share
• High logistics & energy cost
• Dependence on oil imports
• Large informal sector

7. LONG-TERM GROWTH DRIVERS
• Demographic advantage
• Digitization & fintech
• Urbanization
• Infrastructure expansion
• Global export integration

8. MACRO RISKS
• Global recession
• Oil price spikes
• Weak monsoons
• Fiscal slippage
• Banking stress
